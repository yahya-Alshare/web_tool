Download Speed

The download speed is the most important indicator in calculating the speed of the Internet now, so the higher the download speed, the better, watching a video on YouTube without any interruption is the responsibility of the download speed, and downloading files from the Internet at a high speed and a short time is also responsible for the download speed, for example Install applications for your smartphone from the App Store, so the download speed means the speed of obtaining the application from the store. The Internet speed is measured in megabits per second, and every 8 megabyte equals 1 megabyte. For example, you want to download a 10 megabyte file from the Internet, and your download speed is 8 megabits per second, it will take ten seconds to download a 10MB file.

Upload Speed

Usually in Internet connections for most people, the upload speed is lower than the download speed, and the upload speed is responsible for uploading and sending you data to the Internet, and the higher the upload speed, the better, for example, uploading a video to social media or to YouTube is fast if it is Your upload speed is high and vice versa, the upload speed is measured in megabits per second, and people who upload multimedia and work on Internet sites need a high upload speed to get their work done quickly and easily.

Ping response

The lower the ping value, the better it indicates that your connection is better, measured by (ms) i.e. a millisecond, which is part of a thousandth of a second, and the ping process sends a message to the server or another computer and then waits for a response to that message (Packet) This tool is used to measure the speed of the communication response between the two computers or the computer and the server connected via the local network or the Internet. In this case, the ping process is between your computer or smartphone and one of our servers on the Internet.

Jitter

The lower the Jitter value, the better it indicates that your connection is better. It is the delay value between each data packet and another over the network. Jitter is also measured by (ms), i.e. millisecond, which is part of a thousandth of a second. The value is low, but if the network is unstable, the value will be high.
